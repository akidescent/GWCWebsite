Depression

Definition

A mental health disorder is characterized by a persistently depressed mood or loss of interest in activities, causing a significant impairment in one's daily life.

Effects on Women:

Depression is twice as likely to affect women's factors include reproductive hormones, a differing female response to stress, and social pressures that are unique to a woman’s life experiences.

Common forms of depression in women

  • Major Depression
  • -Lose happiness in activities one would find enjoyable before.
  • Postpartum Depression or “Baby blues”
  • -Occurs after a woman gives birth or in the months leading up to giving birth.
  • Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder
  • -Ties in with a woman's menstrual cycle.

How it differentiates between men and women

  • Women feel anxious and scared; men feel guarded
  • Women blame themselves for the depression; men blame others
  • Women commonly feel sad, worthless, and apathetic when depressed; men tend to feel irritable and angry
  • Women are more likely to avoid conflicts when depressed; men are more likely to create conflicts
  • Women feel lethargic and nervous; men feel agitated and restless
  • Women easily talk about their feelings of self-doubt and despair; men hide feelings of self-doubt and despair-considering it a sign of weakness

Symptoms

  • Feelings of emptiness, and sadness
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Suicidal thoughts or attempts of suicide
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Physical symptoms – cramps, breast tenderness
  • Lack of energy
  • Feeling out of control
  • Mood swings and feelings of tearfulness
  • Panic attacks
  • Disinterest in daily activities and relationships

How to seek medical help

  • Consult your doctor if you fear you may have depression
  • Don’t keep your feelings bottled up – find a support group with people you trust

Self-help Options:

While seeking for professional help is recommended, finding self-help options is also very recommended for people coping with depression.

The following are helpful coping mechanisms:

  • Healthy Eating Habits
  • Exercising
  • Have A Healthy Sleeping Schedule
  • Have A Good Support System
  • Do Things That Make You Feel Good
