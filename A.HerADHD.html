ADHD



Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a brain disorder marked by an ongoing pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with life.


How it affects women

 Males tend to have the hyperactive/impulsive kind, which may cause them to be fidgety, interrupt others, have mood swings, and be impatient.

Females have a tendency to exhibit the inattentive type, which makes it hard to focus, pay attention to details, listen, and remember things.

Symptoms:

People with ADHD show a persistent pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with functioning or development: Three types: inattentive, hyperactive/impulsive, and a combination of the two. Inattention: is not being able to carefully complete a task, pay attention, think about, listen to, or watch someone or something. Impulsivity: Acting on sudden desires, ideas, or feelings rather than from careful thought.

Understanding ADHD Symptoms in Women

Here are some ways that ADHD might show up in your life:
  • -Your desk at work is piled high with papers. Even when you make a big effort to tidy it, it only stays clear for a day or two. Being at the office feels difficult. The noise and people make it hard to get work done.
  • -You often stay late at the office, as the only time you can work well is when everyone else has left and it's quiet.


  • -You spend a lot of time and effort to look "normal," and hope no one notices the real you.

Many women are relieved to learn that behaviors they have been struggling with for so long are because of ADHD.

Along with ADHD

When you have more than one condition, they are called comorbid conditions or coexisting conditions. Here are some conditions that women often have in addition to their ADHD:
  • Substance use disorders, such as alcohol or drugs
  • Sleep disorders
  • Eating disorders, such as anorexia or bulimia
  • Mood disorders, like depression or bipolar disorder

How to seek medical health:

If you think you might have ADHD, it's important to be diagnosed by a healthcare professional. An accurate diagnosis and treatment will allow you to get relief from your symptoms and greatly improve the quality of your life.
